# 🎸 Guitar Workshop Simulator

A Roblox simulator game where you build a guitar-making business from the ground up — chop wood, craft guitars, tune them to perfection, and grow your workshop into a renowned brand.

> 🚧 **Status:** In active development. Not yet published.

---

## Concept

Most simulator games on Roblox revolve around the same handful of themes — pets, mining, cooking. **Guitar Workshop Simulator** takes the genre somewhere it hasn't really gone before: lutherie. You start with a small wooden workshop and a single tree, and end up running a full-blown guitar brand producing everything from classic acoustics to custom collector's pieces.

The core loop is simple to learn, satisfying to repeat:

**Chop wood → Craft guitar parts → Assemble → Tune → Sell → Upgrade your workshop → Repeat**

## Features

- **Hands-on wood gathering** — chop down trees with real physics: trunks topple and fall naturally, leaves scatter, and every tree on the map can be tuned independently (how fast it regrows, how much it rewards, how it falls).
- **Guitar crafting** — gather the right materials and assemble real guitar components (body, neck, fretboard, bridge, tuning pegs) into a finished instrument.
- **Tuning mini-game** — get the strings into the right frequency range for a bonus payout. Nail a perfect tune and earn up to 50% more on the sale.
- **Workshop progression** — start in a tiny wooden shed building classic acoustics, and work your way up through electric guitars, basses, and limited collector's models as your workshop grows from a backyard operation into a full-size factory and eventually a luxury brand.
- **An economy that rewards both speed and patience** — sell raw wood for quick cash, or invest it into crafting higher-value guitars for a bigger payout.
- **Experience & leveling** — every action you take in the workshop earns XP, with a clean, satisfying progress bar tracking your growth toward the next level.

## Progression Overview

| Level Range | Workshop Tier | What You Build |
|---|---|---|
| 1–5 | Small wooden workshop | Classic acoustic guitars |
| 6–15 | Mid-size studio | Electric guitars |
| 16–30 | Factory | Bass guitars, custom models |
| 31+ | Luxury brand | Collector's guitars |
